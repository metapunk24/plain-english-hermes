# Quick Checklist: Plain English Self-Review

Use this 20-point checklist before publishing any text written in plain English.

## Structure & Layout
1. **One idea per sentence.** Avoid packing multiple thoughts into a single sentence.
2. **Average sentence length under 20 words.** Break long sentences into two shorter ones.
3. **One topic per paragraph.** Start with the key point; keep paragraphs 2–4 sentences long.
4. **Use vertical lists** for three or more related items, steps, or options.
5. **Logical order.** Present information in the sequence readers need to act on it.

## Voice & Tone
6. **Active voice preferred.** Write "We will send the report" rather than "The report will be sent by us."
7. **Address the reader directly** ("you", "your") to create engagement.
8. **Positive phrasing.** Say what to do, not just what not to do. ("Keep dry" beats "Do not get wet.")
9. **Consistent tone.** Match formality to your audience—avoid sudden shifts.
10. **No unnecessary jargon.** If a specialist term is needed, explain it on first use.

## Words & Phrases
11. **Everyday words first.** Choose "use" over "utilize", "help" over "assist".
12. **Cut redundancy.** Remove phrases like "at this point in time" → "now".
13. **Strong verbs.** Prefer "decide" to "make a decision"; "analyze" to "conduct an analysis".
14. **Concrete nouns.** Name people, places, and things rather than abstract concepts where possible.
15. **No hidden verbs.** "Apply for" not "make an application for"; "discuss" not "have a discussion about".

## Punctuation & Mechanics
16. **Minimal punctuation clutter.** Use commas and dashes only where they aid clarity.
17. **Colon for lists; dash for emphasis.** Reserve semicolons for complex lists.
18. **Consistent capitalization** and spelling (British vs. American chosen and applied uniformly).
19. **Numbers as numerals** for ages, dates, amounts, and statistics ("3 years", "$50").

## Reader Check
20. **Test with a fresh reader.** If someone outside your team can follow the text on first reading without asking questions, it passes.

*Tip: Run your text through a readability formula (see `readability-tools.md`) and aim for a reading age of around 13 for general audiences.*
